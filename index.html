Text, Narration, Image

In this image, we have a lateral view of a human skull. The skull is the bony structure that encases and protects the brain. Looking at this particular view, we can see the brain within the cranial cavity. The brain is divided into several regions, including the cerebrum, cerebellum, and brainstem. Starting from the lower part, we have the brainstem, which is comprised of the medulla oblongata, pons, and midbrain. The brainstem is responsible for many essential functions such as regulating heart rate, breathing, and basic life-sustaining activities. Moving upwards, we can observe the cerebellum, which sits beneath the cerebrum. The cerebellum is primarily responsible for coordination, precision, and voluntary movements. Lastly, we have the cerebrum, the largest and most complex part of the brain. It's divided into two hemispheres and is responsible for higher functions such as thinking, perceiving, and voluntary actions.

The skull is the bony structure that encases and protects the brain. Looking at this particular view, we can see the brain within the cranial cavity. The brain is divided into several regions, including the cerebrum, cerebellum, and brainstem. Starting from the lower part, we have the brainstem, which is comprised of the medulla oblongata, pons, and midbrain. The brainstem is responsible for many essential functions such as regulating heart rate, breathing, and basic life-sustaining activities. Moving upwards, we can observe the cerebellum, which sits beneath the cerebrum. The cerebellum is primarily responsible for coordination, precision, and voluntary movements. Lastly, we have the cerebrum, the largest and most complex part of the brain. It's divided into two hemispheres and is responsible for higher functions such as thinking, perceiving, and voluntary actions.
